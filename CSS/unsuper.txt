In unsupervised market segmentation using clustering, the choice of materials, methods, and algorithms depends on various factors including the nature of the data, the objectives of segmentation, and computational resources available. Here's a general outline of materials, methods, and algorithms commonly used:

### Materials:
1. **Data**: You'll need relevant data for segmentation. This could include demographic data, purchase history, behavioral data, or any other information that can help differentiate customers.

### Methods:
1. **Preprocessing**: Before applying clustering algorithms, preprocessing steps like data cleaning, normalization, and feature engineering may be necessary to ensure data quality and relevance.
   
2. **Clustering Algorithms**: Several clustering algorithms can be employed:
    - **K-Means Clustering**: Partition data into K clusters based on similarities.
    - **Hierarchical Clustering**: Build a tree of clusters where each node represents a cluster.
    - **DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise)**: Identifies clusters of varying shapes and sizes in a dataset of spatial points.
    - **Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM)**: Assumes that the data is generated from a mixture of several Gaussian distributions.
    - **Agglomerative Clustering**: Starts with each data point as a separate cluster and merges clusters based on similarity.
    - **Self-Organizing Maps (SOM)**: Neural network-based method that reduces the dimensions of data while preserving topological properties.
    - **Affinity Propagation**: Automatically chooses the number of clusters based on the data.

3. **Evaluation**: Various metrics like silhouette score, Davies–Bouldin index, or within-cluster sum of squares (WCSS) can be used to evaluate the quality of clustering.

### Algorithms:
1. **K-Means Algorithm**:
   - Initialize K cluster centroids randomly.
   - Assign each data point to the nearest centroid.
   - Recalculate the centroids based on the mean of data points assigned to each cluster.
   - Repeat until convergence (when centroids don't change significantly).

2. **Hierarchical Clustering Algorithm**:
   - Start with each data point as a single cluster.
   - Merge the closest clusters iteratively until only one cluster remains.

3. **DBSCAN Algorithm**:
   - Choose a distance threshold ε and minimum number of points required to form a cluster.
   - Identify core points (points with at least 'minPts' within distance ε).
   - Assign border points to a nearby core point's cluster.
   - Assign noise points to their own cluster.

4. **Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM)**:
   - Assumes data is generated from a mixture of several Gaussian distributions.
   - Uses Expectation-Maximization (EM) algorithm to estimate parameters (mean and covariance) of the Gaussian distributions.

5. **Agglomerative Clustering Algorithm**:
   - Start with each data point as a single cluster.
   - Merge the two closest clusters iteratively until the desired number of clusters is reached.

6. **Self-Organizing Maps (SOM)**:
   - Initialize a grid of neurons, each representing a cluster prototype.
   - Iteratively adjust the prototypes to minimize the difference between input data and prototypes.

7. **Affinity Propagation Algorithm**:
   - Calculate a similarity matrix between data points.
   - Iteratively update 'responsibility' and 'availability' matrices until convergence to find exemplars.

When conducting unsupervised market segmentation, it's crucial to experiment with different algorithms, tune hyperparameters, and evaluate the results to choose the best segmentation approach for your specific business needs.
